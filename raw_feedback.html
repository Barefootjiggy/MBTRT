Adam's Food For Thought:

You know the expression there's nothing to fear but fear itself?

Well, success never comes from our comfort zones. Change comes from feeling a bit uncomfortable.

If we're doing what we've always done, we won't feel any differently because it's comfortable to us.

Discomfort means we're doing things DIFFERENTLY, and therefore we're on the right track.

For example:

Typically, if there is a spread of cookies, and all kinds of indulgent food, we'd dig right in.

The more comfortable thing to do in the moment would be to take a plate, and load up. That's what our old selves would do.

The more uncomfortable thing to do in the moment would be to say, "No thank you. I don't want that. It's not a part of my plan." and walk away thinking, "It's not going to help me look or feel better."

In other words, discomfort is our compass.

So, whenever we have an opportunity to feel uncomfortable, let's take it. That discomfort is a signal to us that we're headed in the right direction. It lets us know we're doing something different.

Going back to there's nothing to fear but fear itself -- too often we give in to the discomfort because we simply fear the sensation of discomfort. The important thing to remember: it's just a feeling.

Like clouds, the feeling will pass. In fact, it's very fleeting. Almost as fleeting as the very thing that is causing us the discomfort.
